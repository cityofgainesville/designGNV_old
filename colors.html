City of Gainesville Color Guidelines

Brand Colors

Teal and navy blue are primary colors. Purple, blue and red are secondary. Please refer to Digital Colors for accessible color palette.



Digital Color Palette

The Digital Color palette is slightly different than the City of Gainesville official brand colors and the Department of Doing brand colors. We have migrated the official colors to similar colors in the USWDS2.0 color system token chart. Each color now has a number associated to it. These numbers will help you attain proper contrast ratios for accessibility and ADA compliance. Please read about the USWDS2.0 Magic Number below for more information.

USWDS2.0 Magic Number

Always use the USWDS2.0 color equivalent of a brand color to ensure appropriate contrast ratios. Link for more information.

We call the difference in grade between any two colors the magic number. Magic numbers have important contrast implications:

      • A magic number of 40+ results in WCAG 2.0 AA Large Text contrast (example: gray-90 and indigo-warm-50v).
      • A magic number of 50+ results in WCAG 2.0 AA contrast or AAA Large Text contrast (example: gray-90 and red-40).
      • A magic number of 70+ results in WCAG 2.0 AAA contrast (example: gray-10 and red-80).
      • Colors of grade 50 result in Section 508 AA contrast against both pure white (grade 0) and pure black (grade 100).

Use USWDS magic numbers to choose accessible color combinations from any palette and color family.

Magic Number Example

Here is an example of how to use it:

Our primary brand color is teal. The USWDS2.0 name for that color is: "mint-cool-30v"

      • Let's imagine you want to use that as the background for a block of text.
      • We know that we want a "Magic Number" to be 70 or above.
      • If we use white font (score of 0) on the teal (score of 30), then the difference between 0 and 30 is 30. 30 does not pass any WCAG accessibility standards.
      • If we use black font (score of 100) on the teal (score of 30), then the difference between 30 and 100 is 70. 70 passes all levels of WCAG accessibility standards.
      • You should use black font on the teal background (even if you like how the white font looks better).
